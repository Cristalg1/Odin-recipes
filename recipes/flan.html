Flan

Description

Flan has been a staple of Mexican and Latin American cuisine since Spaniards brought it to Mexico during the Spanish occupation.

A small slice of this impressively rich, creamy, caramel flan dessert goes a long way!

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 package (8 ounces) cream cheese, softened
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Steps

  1. In a heavy saucepan, cook sugar and water over medium-low heat until melted and golden, about 15 minutes. Brush down crystals on the side of the pan with additional water as necessary.
  2. Quickly pour into an ungreased 2-qt. round baking or souffle dish, tilting to coat the bottom; let stand for 10 minutes.
  3. Preheat oven to 350Â°. In a bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth. Beat in eggs, 1 at a time, until thoroughly combined. Add remaining ingredients; mix well. Pour over caramelized sugar.
  4. Place the dish in a larger baking pan. Pour boiling water into larger pan to a depth of 1 in. Bake until the center is just set (mixture will jiggle), 50-60 minutes.
  5. Remove dish from a larger pan to a wire rack; cool for 1 hour. Refrigerate overnight.
  6. To unmold, run a knife around edge and invert onto a large rimmed serving platter. Cut into wedges or spoon onto dessert plates; spoon sauce over each serving.
